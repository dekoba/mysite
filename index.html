Religious Thoughts

It is important to have a positive meaningful worldviews. Since most people have a religion that is deeply influential in thier world view. The most important ethical concern is that regarding other people. It is critically important to be concerned with the well-being of others and thier position and problems.

In a religious context, it is too easy to dismiss another's struggle as 'gods will' and therefore be absolved of responsibility. It is important instead to take ownership of the state of the world. Although we do not shape the world we inherit when we first arrive in a circumstance, we shape the world that is inherited by the next person, and we have a responsibility to move forward in that regard instead of allowing suffering or disconcern to propagate.

An Online Course Outline

Online Courses are profoundly powerful learning expiriences when handled correctly. They need to be done as a platform of cooperative education in which communication is specifically emphasized. Face-to-face classes benefit from tremendous amounts of nonverbal and other communication, a gap which must be closed and further focused to create an even greater semblence of understanding. Online learning is an equal partnership between the educator and the learner in which each are responsible for the results of the experience. To this end, I believe a platform for online learning should include at least the following elements.

  1. A mechanism by which material can be presented, according to effective learning styles.
  2. A quick-feedback mechanism, utilizing as short of a latency as possible. Max latency of a few hours
  3. Student-driven work wherever possible. If the student does not engage in the partnership this platform fails.
  4. A expandable and sharable collection of notes and resources for learning. Nothing should be off the table.
  5. High Expectations and a means of evaluating learning. Traditional question-answer formats are likely insufficient.

I further believe this is possibly an improvement over traditional face-to-face pedagogies. I am conducting an expiriment in which Ill work with a small group of students in a face-to-face setting to determine thier readiness for this style of learning. If it goes well, I wil expand it into one of my future classes. Furthermore, I am thinking of creating a platform by which I can standardize the supporting efforts of this. The big things I am expecting to put into place:

  1. An overview of the materials from beginning to end. A textbook may work for this but im thinking more of a summary.
  2. A list of assessable goals for the course: at the end, this is how students are evaluated.
  3. I varied list of example assessments. These can be quizzes, essays, teching encounters, programs, artwork, discussions, etc.
  4. A expandable and sharable collection of notes and resources for learning. This will begin slightly populated, and expanded as students add to it. Students will be expected to add to it.
  5. A model indicating a suggested pathway or pathways through the course. Thinking like skill-trees in video games.
